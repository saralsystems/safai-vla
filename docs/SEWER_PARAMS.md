# Indian Sewer Geometry Reference

Parameters sourced from CPHEEO Manual on Sewerage and Sewage Treatment Systems, NEERI reports, and IIT Bombay sewer sludge characterization studies.

## Pipe Geometry

- Minimum municipal sewer diameter: 150mm (lateral), 200-300mm (sub-main)
- Main sewer diameter: 600mm–1200mm (target range for this project)
- Cross-section: circular (most common), rectangular (older construction)
- Material: RCC (reinforced cement concrete), brick masonry, stoneware, corroded CI/DI
- Gradient: 1:100 to 1:500 depending on diameter
- Manhole spacing: 30m (straight) to 15m (curves), per IS 1742

## Sewer Characteristics (Indian-specific)

- Irregular hand-built geometry — not precision-manufactured European pipes
- Brick masonry sections with uneven surfaces
- Root intrusion common
- Structural damage: cracks, joint displacement, spalling
- Mixed-use: storm water and sewage combined in many municipalities
- Debris: construction waste, plastic, textiles, sediment

## Sludge Properties

- Total solids: 2–8% (varies by season, location, upstream load)
- Specific gravity: 1.02–1.05
- Rheology: non-Newtonian (shear-thinning at low shear rates)
- Composition: organic solids, grit, grease, fibrous material
- Hydrogen sulfide: 10–200 ppm (toxic, primary cause of worker death)
- Methane: present in confined spaces (explosion risk)

## Environmental Conditions

- Visibility: near-zero in active sewers (turbid water, no ambient light)
- Temperature: 25–40°C (tropical)
- Humidity: 90–100%
- Camera degradation: water droplets, condensation, sludge splatter on lens
